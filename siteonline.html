Before you get started
You're going to need a few tools:

  • FileZilla or another ftp client
    FileZilla is an open source ftp (file transfer protocol) client that you can use to move files from your computer to your server.
  • jEdit or another text editor
    jEdit is going to be used before you even start the hosting process, because you need to make a website before you can host a website. If you already have a website made you can ignore this requirement.
  • A Website
    While it may seem self-explanatory, but you do need a website before you can put a website online.

Hosting with 000webhost
The reason we use 000freehosting is simply because they're free. If you happen to be willing to spend money or want professional hosting, do so. I can suggest MediaTemple and StuzzHosting to anyone looking for professional hosting.

  1. Go to 000's site and sign up for an account and hosting. It make take a minute or two to be processed and ready to go
    Right now you don't have a domain (unless you set one up separately), so just use one of their second level domains.
  2. Once you've been processed, enter your control panel, the important things are all either in your head or in the sidebar.
    Copy the Username (it's a followed by a string of numbers).
  3. Open FileZilla if you don't already have it open, and click on the site's icon (the first icon in the toolbar). You should see the Site Manager.
    You can come back to this later for other sites you setup too. It also has the ability to change FileZilla settings just for one site.
  4. The Host is your site's address
    Leave the Port blank
    The Server Type is FTP - File Transfer Protocol
    The Logon Type is Normal
    The User is the username you copied earlier
    And the Password is your chosen password
    Click on the "New Site" button.
    The settings are to the left.
    After you fill in those settings, go into the Advanced tab, and find the Default Remote Directory option, and set it to /publicftp. You're doing this because it will make sure that when you connect to the site, you're in the right remote directory (the one which holds the files for your site).
  5. Don't close out of the Site Manager, but click Connect down at the bottom. This will connect you to your site.
    To connect to your site again, click on the same icon, choose your site, and click connect. If you're on windows (I don't know about OSX), you'll have a drop down button next to the icon, click it and your site to connect, sorry Linux users.
  6. Now navigate to your site, you should see the site you just uploaded (unless you don't have your home page named index.html, index.php, index.htm, or default in place of index).
